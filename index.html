Injury Prevention

Stretching, mobility, speed preservation & tips for a soccer player with cervical hernias

⚡ Protecting & Building Your Speed
Don't build bulky legs
Heavy, high-volume quad work adds mass that slows acceleration. The lower body program here keeps volume and load intentionally moderate - enough to strengthen, not enough to bulk.
Train explosiveness, not just strength
Once Phase 2 begins, consider adding 2-3 sets of box jumps or broad jumps before your lower body session (when fresh). Keep it low volume - 3x5 is enough. Don't do these on tired legs.
Hip flexor strength = sprint speed
Weak hip flexors are the most underrated limiter of top speed in soccer players. The hanging leg raises in the program address this directly every single session.
Hamstring strength protects speed
Nordic Curls build eccentric hamstring strength - the exact quality that prevents hamstring tears at full sprint. These are in the program from Phase 1 and should never be skipped.
Don't run yourself flat in Phase 3
Keep at least one run as short sprints or strides (6-8x80m at 85-90% effort with full recovery). This maintains fast-twitch fiber recruitment that long slow runs gradually erode.
🌅 Daily Mobility Routine (10-15 min) - every morning
Hip Flexor Stretch (kneeling)
Daily
2x45s per side
Rear knee on ground, drive hips forward. Squeeze glute of rear leg. Critical for soccer players - tight hip flexors reduce stride length and strain the lower back.
90/90 Hip Stretch
Daily
2x45s per side
Sit on floor with both legs at 90 degrees. Lean over front shin. Opens hip rotation range used most in cutting and turning at speed.
Thoracic Rotation (seated)
Daily
2x10 reps/side
Sit cross-legged, hands behind head, rotate torso. When thoracic spine stiffens, the cervical spine compensates and takes more stress.
World's Greatest Stretch
Pre-workout
5 reps/side
Lunge forward, place same-side hand on ground, rotate opposite arm to ceiling. Combines hip flexor, thoracic, and hamstring mobility in one movement.
Standing Hamstring Stretch
Daily
2x40s per side
Foot on low surface, slight forward hinge at hip. Hamstring flexibility directly influences sprint mechanics and reduces injury risk at top speed.
Calf & Achilles Stretch
Daily
2x40s per side
Against wall: straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for Achilles. Soccer players are notorious for neglecting the Achilles - 90 seconds daily is worth it.
🔥 Pre-Workout Activation (5 min) - before every session
Glute Bridge
Pre-workout
2x15 reps
Activates glutes before lower body work. Dormant glutes shift load to hamstrings and lower back.
Scapular Push-ups
Pre-workout
2x12 reps
Protract and retract scapula without bending elbows. Primes the shoulder before pressing and protects the cervical chain.
Chin Tucks
Neck - always
2x15 reps
Always before upper body. Resets cervical posture and activates deep neck flexors before loading the shoulder girdle.
Arm Circles + Shoulder CARs
Pre-workout
10 reps each direction
Slow controlled articular rotations through full shoulder range. Reduces impingement risk during pressing.
🧊 Recovery Tips
🛁
Cold shower after intense sessions
2-3 minutes cold water after Phase 2 and 3 sessions. Reduces inflammation and speeds recovery.
😴
Sleep is the best recovery tool
7.5-9 hours. Growth hormone peaks in the first 90 minutes of deep sleep. Even 1 hour less measurably reduces next-day strength output.
🦵
Foam roll 2-3x per week
Focus on calves and lateral quad. Reduces stiffness and maintains tissue quality across the 10 weeks.
🚶
Walk on rest days
20-30 minutes easy walking keeps blood flowing, reduces soreness, adds no meaningful fatigue.
📅
Deload week - Week 5
Drop all sets by 40%, remove heaviest compound movements. Same exercises, same frequency. You will feel stronger in week 6.

Neck Safety Protocol

Non-negotiable throughout all 10 weeks.

01No overhead pressing behind the neck - ever
02Avoid heavy axial barbell loading - use goblet squat, safety bar, or leg press if symptoms arise
03No extreme neck flexion or extension under load
04Cable Face Pulls every single session - most protective exercise for cervical discs
05If any tingling or shooting pain in arm/shoulder, stop immediately - not after the set
06Supported rows preferred over free-standing bent-over rows
07Lat Pulldown: front only - never behind the neck
08Sleep with a cervical support pillow
